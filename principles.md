# TIG Principles

These principles are non-negotiable.

Every feature, collector, algorithm and AI model must follow them.

---

# Principle 1

## Evidence Before AI

AI is never the source of truth.

Evidence is.

Every recommendation must be traceable back to evidence.

---

# Principle 2

## Explain Everything

Users must always be able to answer:

Why did TIG recommend this?

No black-box scoring.

---

# Principle 3

## Public Evidence Only

TIG only uses legally obtainable information.

No private data.

No unethical collection.

No guessing.

---

# Principle 4

## Collect Once, Use Everywhere

Evidence is industry agnostic.

One hiring signal may benefit:

- Recruiters
- Payroll
- Insurance
- Cybersecurity
- Marketing

Collectors should never be built for one industry.

---

# Principle 5

## AI Explains

AI does not determine intent.

AI explains intent.

The Intent Engine remains deterministic.

---

# Principle 6

## Configurable Intelligence

Business rules belong in data.

Not code.

Weights.

Thresholds.

Confidence.

Freshness.

Everything should be configurable.

---

# Principle 7

## Every Opportunity Needs Evidence

Every recommendation should expose its evidence chain.

Example:

Funding

↓

Hiring Spike

↓

Office Expansion

↓

High Recruitment Intent

---

# Principle 8

## Confidence Matters

Every evidence item contains:

Source Reliability

Evidence Confidence

Intent Confidence

These are separate concepts.

---

# Principle 9

## Everything Decays

Evidence loses value over time.

Funding from three years ago is less important than funding from last week.

Freshness is part of the scoring model.

---

# Principle 10

## Modular Architecture

Collectors are independent.

Playbooks are independent.

Industries are independent.

The engine should grow by adding modules—not rewriting existing code.

---

# Principle 11

## Human-in-the-Loop

Users should be able to provide feedback:

- Useful
- Not Useful
- Converted
- False Positive

The engine should learn from outcomes without sacrificing explainability.

---

# Principle 12

## Trust Is the Product

The dashboard is not the product.

The AI is not the product.

Trust is the product.

If users trust TIG's recommendations, everything else follows.
