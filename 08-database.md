# TIG Core Database Design

Version

v0.1

---

# Purpose

The TIG database stores all business intelligence generated by the platform.

It is designed around domains rather than individual tables.

The database should remain scalable, explainable and extensible.

Business intelligence must always remain independent from platform operations.

---

# Database Philosophy

The database follows several core principles.

- Evidence is immutable.
- Relationships are first-class citizens.
- Business rules belong in configuration.
- No duplicated business logic.
- Every record is traceable.
- Everything has ownership.

---

# Versioning

Business records are append-only where possible.

Evidence is never updated.

Signals may be regenerated.

Intent Scores are recalculated.

Opportunities may expire.

Historical records remain available for auditing.

---

# Indexing Strategy

Primary indexes should exist on:

- company_id
- evidence_id
- signal_id
- playbook_id
- observed_at
- category
- collector

Additional indexes may be added based on query performance.

# Design Principles

The database should support:

✓ Unlimited companies

✓ Unlimited evidence

✓ Unlimited signals

✓ Unlimited playbooks

✓ Unlimited collectors

✓ Unlimited users

without structural redesign.

---

# Summary

The TIG database is designed as a domain-driven, modular intelligence store.

Business intelligence remains isolated from platform concerns and operational infrastructure.

This separation allows TIG to scale from a simple SaaS application to a full commercial intelligence platform without requiring major architectural changes.
